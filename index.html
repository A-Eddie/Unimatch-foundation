Who We Are

Built for Kenya.
Designed for the future.

Unimatch Nexus Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to bridging the educational divide in Kenya. Through scholarships, mentorship, skills training, and community programs, we empower young Kenyans to realize their full potential.

Rooted in the belief that every child deserves access to quality education regardless of their background, we operate across all 47 counties of Kenya, partnering with schools, universities, government agencies, and corporate partners to create lasting change.

Scholarship Fund

A scholarship does more than pay fees. It protects a student's promise at the exact moment poverty threatens to interrupt it.

Across Kenya, many gifted students sit in classrooms carrying a burden no child should have to carry. They are bright, disciplined, and hungry to learn, yet every term begins with uncertainty: Will the fees be paid? Will they be sent home? Will they have books, uniform, transport, exam registration, or a quiet place to study? For these learners, talent is present, but opportunity is fragile. The Scholarship Fund exists to make sure financial hardship does not become the reason a capable young person drops out of school.

Unimatch Nexus Foundation identifies promising students from marginalized households, rural communities, informal settlements, and families affected by hardship. We look beyond grades alone and consider character, consistency, leadership potential, and the student's personal circumstances. Once selected, scholars receive support for tuition, learning materials, exam preparation, mentorship, and welfare follow-up. This approach matters because a scholarship that only pays one bill may not be enough. A student also needs encouragement, accountability, and practical support through the pressure points that often decide whether they stay in school.

For donors, this program offers one of the clearest pathways from generosity to measurable change. A contribution can keep a student in class for a term, prepare a candidate for national exams, help a learner transition to college, or protect a family from choosing between education and survival. The return is not abstract. It is seen in improved attendance, stronger performance, renewed confidence, and the dignity of a young person who knows someone believes in their future.

When you support the Scholarship Fund, you are not only funding education. You are defending potential, strengthening families, and helping Kenya build a generation of graduates who can lead, serve, innovate, and lift others after them.

Your gift funds: school fees, uniforms, books, meals, exam support, and student welfare checks.

Youth Mentorship

Many young people have ambition, but ambition grows stronger when it is guided by wisdom, exposure, and trusted encouragement.

In many communities, students are told to work hard, but few are shown what that hard work can become. A learner may dream of becoming a doctor, engineer, lawyer, teacher, designer, entrepreneur, or public servant, yet never meet someone who has walked that path. Without guidance, career choices can feel distant and confusing. Without role models, confidence can shrink. Without someone to ask honest questions, students may make decisions based on fear, pressure, or limited information.

The Youth Mentorship program closes that gap by connecting students with professionals, university students, entrepreneurs, community leaders, and trained volunteers who can offer practical guidance. Our mentorship cycles combine career talks, life-skills sessions, academic planning, leadership development, personal discipline, and one-on-one check-ins. Students learn how to set goals, manage time, communicate clearly, prepare for interviews, choose subjects, apply for opportunities, and build the character required to succeed beyond the classroom.

This program is especially powerful because mentorship changes how a young person sees themselves. A student who once believed that success belonged to other people begins to understand that their own future is possible. They learn that challenges can be managed, mistakes can become lessons, and their background does not have to limit their destination. A mentor does not replace a teacher or parent; a mentor adds another circle of support around a learner who is trying to grow.

For donors, Youth Mentorship is an investment in direction. Your support helps us train mentors, organize workshops, transport students to exposure visits, prepare career materials, and follow up with mentees throughout the year. It turns professional knowledge into community capital. It gives students access to conversations that can change decisions, strengthen resilience, and open doors they did not know existed.

When donors stand behind mentorship, they help create confident young leaders who are not only educated, but guided, grounded, and ready to contribute.

Your gift funds: mentor training, student workshops, career materials, transport, and monitoring.

Digital Skills Lab

Digital access should not depend on where a student was born, what their school can afford, or whether a computer lab exists nearby.

Technology is reshaping education, work, business, communication, and public life. Yet many students in underserved communities finish school with very little practical exposure to digital tools. Some have never used a laptop for research. Some have never created a presentation, typed a professional document, built a simple website, or learned how online platforms can support learning and income. This digital divide does not reflect a lack of intelligence. It reflects unequal access.

The Digital Skills Lab brings hands-on technology learning to schools and communities that are often left behind. We teach digital literacy, typing, research skills, online safety, coding basics, design thinking, presentation skills, responsible internet use, and problem-solving through technology. Students do not only listen; they build. They create simple websites, posters, school projects, data tables, pitch decks, and digital portfolios. Each session is designed to make technology feel practical, useful, and connected to real opportunities.

Our goal is not to turn every learner into a software engineer. Our goal is to make sure every learner has the confidence to participate in a digital world. A student who understands technology can research scholarships, apply for jobs, support a family business, start freelancing, communicate professionally, and imagine solutions for problems in their own community. Digital skill becomes a bridge between classroom learning and the future economy.

For donors, this program offers a high-impact way to expand opportunity at scale. One laptop can train many students. One lab day can introduce an entire classroom to skills they may use for years. One sponsored trainer can help learners discover talents that were hidden only because they lacked access. Your support helps us provide devices, internet, facilitators, learning materials, software tools, and safe digital learning spaces.

By supporting the Digital Skills Lab, donors help prepare young Kenyans not only to consume technology, but to create with it, work with it, and use it to build better futures.

Your gift funds: laptops, internet bundles, trainers, software tools, and school-based lab days.

Girls in STEM

When girls are welcomed into science, technology, engineering, and mathematics, entire communities gain stronger futures.

Too many girls grow up hearing, directly or quietly, that STEM subjects are not for them. Some lack encouragement at home. Some attend schools without enough lab resources. Some miss learning time because of poverty, household duties, or lack of sanitary support. Others have never met a woman scientist, engineer, technologist, doctor, researcher, or founder who looks like them and understands where they come from. These barriers can make brilliant girls doubt themselves before they even have the chance to try.

Girls in STEM is designed to replace doubt with confidence, exposure, and practical experience. We create safe and inspiring learning spaces where girls can ask questions, experiment, build, lead, and imagine bold careers. The program combines hands-on science activities, coding introductions, mentorship from women professionals, school clubs, innovation challenges, career talks, lab visits, and confidence-building sessions. We also pay attention to the real-life issues that affect girls' participation, including dignity, safety, guidance, and consistent encouragement.

This work matters because STEM is not only about subjects. It is about power, participation, and possibility. Girls who gain confidence in STEM learn to solve problems, analyze information, use tools, speak with authority, and see themselves as creators of knowledge. They become better prepared for careers in health, engineering, agriculture, climate solutions, technology, research, education, and entrepreneurship. When one girl discovers that she belongs in these spaces, she often becomes a model for her sisters, classmates, and neighbors.

For donors, Girls in STEM is a direct investment in equity and national development. Your support helps us provide STEM kits, transport to exposure visits, role-model forums, sanitary support, club materials, trainers, and innovation activities. These resources turn encouragement into action. They help a girl move from watching from the edge of the room to leading from the front.

Supporting this program means helping girls claim space in fields that will shape Kenya's future. It means funding confidence, competence, and courage in young women whose ideas deserve to be seen and supported.

Your gift funds: STEM kits, role-model forums, sanitary support, lab visits, and girls' innovation clubs.

Rural Outreach

Distance should never decide whether a child receives support, guidance, learning resources, or a fair chance to dream.

In rural areas, education challenges can be quiet but heavy. A student may walk long distances to school, study without enough books, miss exposure to career guidance, or learn in a classroom where teachers are doing their best with limited resources. Parents may value education deeply but lack the means to provide materials, transport, digital access, or exam support. Many communities have bright young people whose potential remains unseen because opportunity rarely travels far enough to meet them.

Rural Outreach exists to bring support closer to the learners and families who need it most. Our teams work with schools, local leaders, parents, churches, youth groups, and community-based partners to identify needs and deliver practical interventions. We organize learning days, mentorship visits, resource drives, community forums, academic motivation sessions, digital exposure, and follow-up visits for vulnerable learners. The program is built on respect: we do not arrive with assumptions; we listen, partner, and respond to the realities of each community.

This approach matters because rural transformation is not achieved through one event alone. It requires trust, consistency, and local ownership. When students see visitors return, when parents are included in conversations, and when schools receive resources that match real needs, support becomes more sustainable. Outreach also helps us identify students who may qualify for scholarships, mentorship, digital skills training, or other foundation programs. In this way, Rural Outreach becomes the doorway through which hidden talent is discovered and connected to wider opportunity.

For donors, this program offers a meaningful way to reach learners who are often missing from the spotlight. Your support funds travel, learning materials, community meetings, school supplies, resource centers, facilitator costs, and follow-up systems. It helps opportunity move beyond urban centers and into villages, remote schools, and households where encouragement can change a child's path.

When donors support Rural Outreach, they help ensure that geography does not become destiny. They help build communities where students feel remembered, parents feel included, and schools feel strengthened.

Your gift funds: outreach travel, learning materials, community forums, resource centers, and follow-up visits.

Entrepreneurship Hub

Kenya's young people are not only job seekers. With the right support, they become builders of local solutions.

Many young people carry business ideas shaped by the problems they see every day. They notice gaps in agriculture, education, transport, fashion, digital services, food, recycling, health access, and community trade. What they often lack is not creativity. They lack training, seed capital, mentorship, market knowledge, financial discipline, and the confidence to test an idea in the real world. Without support, many promising ideas remain conversations, sketches, or abandoned dreams.

The Entrepreneurship Hub helps youth turn ideas into practical ventures. We provide business training, financial literacy, customer discovery, market research, branding basics, pricing guidance, record keeping, pitching practice, mentorship, and early-stage incubation. Participants learn how to define a problem, understand customers, build simple prototypes, manage money, communicate value, and improve their ideas through feedback. The Hub also creates a community where young founders can learn from one another instead of struggling alone.

This program is important because entrepreneurship is both an economic and social pathway. A young person who starts a small venture can earn income, support family members, create jobs, solve local problems, and become a role model in their community. Even when a participant does not immediately launch a business, the skills gained are valuable: initiative, communication, planning, resilience, financial responsibility, and problem-solving. These are the same skills that make young people stronger employees, leaders, and citizens.

For donors, the Entrepreneurship Hub provides a powerful way to invest in self-reliance and innovation. Your support funds training sessions, business coaches, seed grants, pitch days, prototype materials, workspace access, follow-up mentorship, and incubation support. A modest grant can help a youth test a product, buy essential tools, register a business, reach customers, or move from informal effort to structured growth.

When donors support the Hub, they help young Kenyans build income with dignity. They help transform raw ideas into enterprises that can serve communities, create opportunity, and prove that local challenges can be answered by local talent.

Your gift funds: seed grants, business coaching, pitch days, prototype materials, and incubation support.
